Roger’s Enterprises — Early Black Friday “Today Only”

As acting Creative Director on Roger’s Black Friday program, I pushed for an early “Today Only” launch that unified email, web, push, SMS, social, TV, and radio. The goal was to claim the conversation before competitors and concentrate demand into a single, high-impact day.

Desktop hero — simplified messaging and a single CTA for faster decision-making.

My role

  • Owned campaign strategy, creative direction, and QA.
  • Partnered with merchandising and finance to align hero offer and inventory.
  • Led copy + design for email, homepage hero, and supporting digital placements.
  • Coordinated with media teams (radio, TV, social) for a single message across channels.

Business impact

  • +21% lift in conversion vs. prior Black Friday.
  • +$980K incremental revenue attributed to the “Today Only” push.
  • Higher AOV driven by clear framing of doorbusters and add-on categories.
  • Reduced decision fatigue by focusing on one hero and a single, bold CTA.

Hypothesis

If we pulled Black Friday forward with a strong single-day hook and cleaned up the offer hierarchy, we could win share of wallet early and lock in high-value buyers before competitors’ promotions hit inboxes.

What I optimized

  • Compressed the creative into one hero image and one CTA instead of a cluttered grid.
  • Tightened copy to emphasize urgency (“Today Only”) and doorbuster scarcity.
  • Ensured continuity between email hero, homepage, and paid media so shoppers never “lost the thread.”

Why it worked

The campaign removed friction: customers saw the same hero image and message everywhere they looked. That confidence and clarity translated into faster decisions and higher cart values.

Urgency: Doorbusters end at midnight—limited sizes move fast.

Value: One bold hero + single CTA simplified decisions.

Connection: Treat-yourself energy with gift-worthy value.

Conversions increased by +21%. Incremental revenue increased by +$980K.

I loved calling the early launch and watching revenue spike in real time.
